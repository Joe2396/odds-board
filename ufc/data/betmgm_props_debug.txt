MMA BETTING ODDS By default, MMA betting odds are displayed as a fraction, but you can change this to either the decimal or moneyline (American) format. However you choose to display them, these odds reflect the general consensus of how likely or unlikely a fighter is to win. A fighter with 1/1 odds has a perceived equal chance of winning or losing a fight. With odds higher (e.g. 1/4) than this denoting an underdog and odds under this (e.g. 4/1) identifying the favourite. Your challenge is to determine which outcome you think is more likely and whether you agree with the oddsmakers. If you choose an underdog, your payout will be greater if you win, but the perceived likelihood of receiving that payout is lower. Odds also shift over time, especially in the few days before a fight, as more information about the fighters’ health and form is made available. This can mean that betting early gets you a bigger return, especially where you believe one side is being overlooked. POPULAR MMA BETTING MARKETS There are several different ways to bet on MMA. They may not all be available for each fight, but you can expect the most odds to be offered for the biggest and most high-profile fights. Here are some of the most common types of mixed martial arts betting markets. Bout Betting - Sometimes called MMA betting lines, bout betting is the simplest form of wagering on combat sports and involves picking the outcome of the bout, with the options of either fighter or a draw. Rounds Betting - Bouts are split into rounds. In some fights, you can also bet on how many of these rounds will be fought by picking which round the fight will be won in. Method of Victory - In some MMA bouts, you can also place a wager on how the winner will win, either by knockout, technical knockout, disqualification or decision. Parlay/Accumulator - A special type of bet that combines multiple wagers into a single slip (more on these below). MMA ACCUMULATORS Accumulators are a popular choice for those betting on MMA fights as they provide a way to test your MMA knowledge and create the prospect of larger wins. They work by making multiple wagers on a single betting slip, such as picking the winners of several MMA fights from a single fight card. Accumulators offer much higher odds, meaning larger payouts but a lower likelihood of winning. To make an accumulator at BetMGM UK, add all the wagers you want to your betting slip and select the Combination option, enter your bet amount, and click on Place Bet.
