FIFA World Cup 2026 Betting & Odds

The summer of 2026 sees the return of the world's biggest sporting event, the FIFA World Cup. The expanded tournament features a record-breaking 48 teams and 104 games, played across 16 cities and 3 countries, including the USA, Canada, and Mexico. What is the FIFA World Cup 2026? 




Being known as the crown jewel of football, the FIFA World Cup is a tournament that comes around every 4 years, and typically takes place over the summer. This time, there are three host nations, and these are the United States, Mexico, and Canada.




The 2026 World Cup will feature 48 teams, forming 12 groups of 4. The top two from each group will progress to the later rounds, and the eight best third-placed teams from all groups also advance. Here at BOYLE Sports, every single game throughout the tournament will be available to place bets on, with competitive odds and a range of betting markets available.




When is the FIFA 2026 World Cup? 




As many of the players in the World Cup are part of top clubs, this tournament takes place across the summer, when the top leagues have an extended break. The 2026 World Cup will take place from the 11th of June to the 19th of July, meaning it does not affect other betting markets such as Premier League betting, as they take place at different times. Here are the confirmed dates from the FIFA World Cup 2026:




Tournament Start - June 11th
Round of 32  - June 28th
Round of 16 - July 4th
Quarter Finals - July 9th
Semi Finals - July 14th
World Cup Final - July 19th




Despite the World Cup not starting till June 11th, the market is open for pre-tournament odds and betting, with markets like outrights, top goalscorers and group qualification. Typically, the odds are at their most valuable before the tournament begins, as squads have not been finalised, and factors such as injuries could affect how a country performs.




Who are the Favourites for the World Cup 2026 Tournament? 




Despite football outright betting changing all the time, there are a select few teams in the World Cup that look set to challenge for the trophy. Each of these teams has some of the best players in the world, making them countries to look out for when placing outright bets in the World Cup.




Spain 




Being one of the most favoured teams in the tournament, Spain is coming off the back of winning the Euro 2024 competition and sits near the top of the FIFA World Rankings. Their entire squad is full of world-class players, including star Lamine Yamal, Ballon d'Or winner Rodri, and top-class midfielder Pedri. By blending technical quality and defensive structure, they have already proven they can win international tournaments, making them one of the favourites to win the World Cup.




France 




In the last World Cup, France were beaten by Argentina through penalties in the final, and are looking to rectify the recent heartbreak. Mbappe is top of the World Cup top goalscorer markets, and for good reason, as he was able to win the golden boot in the last tournament in Qatar. With the superstar leading the line, France also boasts an incredibly strong squad, with plenty of depth and quality throughout both attack and defence. France is one of the clear favourites for the World Cup, and bets can be placed here in our sports betting markets.




England




Thomas Tuchel’s first major tournament with the Three Lions takes place in North America, and England fans are hoping that the German can take them back to World Cup glory. With top-quality players such as Harry Kane and Jude Bellingham, England might not be as strong as nations like France and Spain, but they have finished with a semi-final and final in their last two major tournaments, meaning they are usually still in action when it comes to the back end of the competition.




Brazil




Carlo Ancelotti is known to be one of the most iconic and respected managers in the World, and he takes over Brazil as he looks to give the country their 6th triumph in the World Cup. Despite the nation not being as good as they have been in the past, they still have some top-quality players, including Vinicius Jr and Raphinha. Whilst the defence may not be as impressive as the attack, Brazil are tipped as one of the teams that has a realistic outside chance to win the tournament.




Argentina




Finally, there is the team that won the last World Cup, Argentina. Despite maybe not being as strong as they were in the 2022 tournament, Argentina is full of established, top-quality players, such as Lautaro Martinez, Cristian Romero and of course, Lionel Messi. Being one of the outside shouts similar to Brazil, Argentina cannot be written off when it comes to outright World Cup betting.
