Group A Odds And Betting Markets For The World Cup 2026
Group A odds

Group A at the 2026 World Cup sees hosts Mexico take centre stage as they look to make home advantage count on football’s biggest stage. Backed by passionate home support and extensive tournament experience, Mexico enter the group as favourites to progress to the knockout rounds.

South Korea arrive as dangerous challengers thanks to their pace, organisation and strong recent tournament pedigree, while Czech Republic bring a disciplined European style capable of troubling any side in the group. South Africa complete the line-up as determined outsiders looking to cause an upset and make a statement on the global stage.

With qualification spots up for grabs and little room for error in the expanded tournament format, Group A could prove to be one of the most competitive opening groups of the World Cup.

Group A betting

The main betting focus in Group A centres around the group winner and teams to qualify markets.

Mexico’s home advantage makes them strong contenders to top the group, while Switzerland are expected to provide the toughest challenge for first place. Egypt’s attacking quality gives them every chance of pushing for qualification, while New Zealand may appeal to bettors searching for value in match betting and outsider markets.

Key fixtures between the leading sides are likely to play a major role in determining how the group unfolds.

Who are the favourites to win Group A?

Mexico are widely considered the favourites to win Group A, with home support and tournament experience expected to give them a major advantage. South Korea and Czech Republic are viewed as the main challengers for qualification, while South Africa will aim to upset the odds and progress to the knockout stages.

When are Group A's matches at the 2026 World Cup?

Group A matches take place between 11 June and 21 June 2026, with all kick-off times in BST:

Mexico vs South Africa – 11 June (20:00 BST)
South Korea vs Czech Republic – 12 June (03:00 BST)
Czech Republic vs South Africa (Thursday, 18 June - 17:00 BST)
Mexico vs South Korea (Friday, 19 June - 02:00 BST)
Czech Republic vs Mexico (Thursday, 25 June - 02:00 BST)
